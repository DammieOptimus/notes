Understanding the Top Up and Get Reward (TGR) Business Model: A Comprehensive Overview

1. What is mentorship all about?

Mentorship within TGR appears to be centred around guidance and support from more experienced members, particularly uplines, to help new members succeed in the business. The uplines possess "business secrets" that can make a new member's journey easier. The idea is that those who have already achieved success in TGR can guide newcomers on how to build their network and understand the business model effectively. This guidance likely involves sharing strategies for reaching out to potential recruits, explaining the benefits of TGR, and helping downlines understand the compensation plan and how to maximise their earnings. Furthermore, new members are added to registered members' training groups where they can learn ways to grow their business. This collective learning environment, facilitated by more experienced members, constitutes a form of mentorship.

2. What are the human relations of the business?

The human relations aspect of TGR is heavily focused on network building through referrals and team collaboration. The business model encourages members to share the opportunity with their friends, family, colleagues, and even strangers. This sharing is not just about recruiting; it's about creating a network where each member's activity can contribute to the earnings of others in their upline. A lot of emphasis is placed on the importance of building a team and teaching them how to do the same. The referral system creates a chain of relationships, with uplines incentivised to support their downlines as their success contributes to the upline's passive income through transaction commissions and indirect bonuses. The business thrives on these interconnected relationships and the principle of "multiplying people" to see "money multiplying itself". The testimonials also highlight a sense of community and shared success within the TGR network.

3. What are the details of the business?

Top Up and Get Reward (TGR) is a telecommunications platform founded in 2018 that allows members to earn money from services they and others use daily, such as airtime, data, cable TV subscriptions, and electricity bill payments.

Key details of the business include:

  • VTU Portal: Upon registration, members receive a personal Virtual Top Up (VTU) portal to conduct transactions and manage their business. This portal allows for recharging airtime and data, subscribing for cable TV, paying electricity bills, funding the wallet, transferring funds to other TGR partners, withdrawing earnings, and monitoring transactions and team members.
  • Registration Packages: TGR offers various registration packages ranging from ₦5,000 (Pearl) to ₦100,000 (Executive Diamond). Higher packages generally offer more levels of commission and access to higher potential earnings and incentives. A 10% cashback is received on the franchise fee upon registration or upgrade.
  • Earning Mechanisms:
    • Personal Usage Cashback: Members earn 2% - 2.5% cashback on their own airtime and data purchases, 0.5% on cable TV subscriptions, and 1% on electricity bill payments.
    • Direct Referral Commissions: Members earn a 22% commission on the franchise fee of those they directly register. Specific amounts and Point Values (PV) vary based on the package the new member chooses.
    • Indirect Registration Bonuses: Members earn 5% - 1.5% bonuses when their downlines register others, spanning multiple levels depending on the member's own package.
    • Transaction Commissions: Members on higher packages (Diamond and Executive Diamond) can earn 0.10% to 0.15% commission on the airtime and data purchases of their team members down to several levels. They also earn 0.10% on their team members' cable TV subscriptions.
    • Incentive Awards: As members and their teams generate Point Value (PV) through registrations, they become eligible for incentives like a ₦750,000 international trip fund, ₦5 million and ₦6 million car funds, and a ₦10 million house fund. A ₦120,000 monthly leadership bonus is also available for consistently generating 10,000 PV.
  • Sustainability and Legitimacy: TGR is registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) and licensed by the Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC). The company has been operating for six years and will be 7 years old by November, indicating a level of stability and a viable business model based on essential and recurring services.
  • No Product Selling (in the traditional sense): The focus is on leveraging everyday transactions rather than selling physical products, which is highlighted as an advantage over traditional product-based network marketing.

4. What are the obstacles or challenges of the business?

While the information shared so far present a largely positive view of TGR, some potential obstacles or challenges can be inferred:

  • Network Building Efforts: Despite the ease of the "product" being essential services, building a successful network still requires effort in reaching out to people, explaining the concept, and convincing them to join. Some individuals may find "getting people" to be challenging.
  • Understanding the Business Model: Network marketing, in general, can sometimes be misunderstood or viewed with skepticism. Educating potential members that TGR is not just about selling recharge cards and highlighting the passive income potential and other benefits is crucial.
  • Maintaining Team Engagement and Growth: Like any network marketing model, sustaining momentum and ensuring the continued activity and growth of one's downline can be an ongoing challenge. Members need to train and support their teams effectively.
  • Competition from Other Telecom Service Providers and Platforms: While TGR offers a unique earning opportunity, it operates within a competitive telecommunications market with established players like MTN, Glo, Airtel, and 9mobile, as well as various other platforms for recharging and bill payments.
  • Overcoming Negative Perceptions of Network Marketing: Most people perceive networkers as broke and that the industry's integrity has been affected by failed companies. Overcoming such negative perceptions might be a hurdle in recruiting. People don't know that TGR is here to stay.
  • Initial Investment (Activation Fee): While entry packages start relatively low, the need for an initial activation fee might be an obstacle for some individuals.

5. How to make profit?

Profit can be made with TGR through multiple avenues:

  • Cashback on Personal Transactions: By using their TGR VTU portal for their own airtime, data, cable TV, and electricity bill payments, members receive instant cashback, effectively reducing their expenses.
  • Referral Commissions: Earning a significant percentage (22%) of the registration fee of each person directly referred and who activates their account provides an immediate profit.
  • Indirect Referral Bonuses: As the network grows and downlines register new members, uplines earn passive bonuses on these registrations, further contributing to profit.
  • Transaction Commissions from Team: Members on higher packages earn a small percentage of the value of airtime, data, cable TV, and electricity bill transactions conducted by their downline members. This creates a stream of passive income based on the collective activity of the team.
  • Incentive Awards: Achieving PV milestones through team growth unlocks substantial rewards like travel funds, car funds, and a house fund, which can be considered significant forms of profit or financial gain.
  • Leadership Bonus: Consistent generation of 10,000 PV per month yields a leadership bonus of ₦120,000, providing a regular income stream for active leaders.
  • Providing VTU Services to Others: Members can use their VTU portal to sell airtime, data, etc., to non-members, potentially adding a service charge for profit.

The key to maximising profit in TGR is to build a large and active network where both direct and indirect referrals contribute to registration bonuses and the ongoing transactions of team members generate passive income. Upgrading to higher packages unlocks more levels of these passive earnings and eligibility for higher incentives.

6. What problems are solved?

TGR aims to solve several problems for its members:

  • Lack of Financial Return on Essential Spending: It addresses the issue of individuals spending significant amounts of money on airtime, data, and bills without receiving any financial benefit in return. TGR allows members to earn from these necessary expenses through cashback.
  • Limited Income Opportunities for the Average Person: TGR provides a platform for individuals to create an additional income stream and potentially achieve financial freedom through network building and leveraging the large telecommunications market. The low entry barrier (starting from ₦5,000) makes this opportunity accessible to a wide range of people.
  • Lack of Passive Income Sources: The business model offers the potential for passive income through commissions on team members' transactions, allowing individuals to earn even when they are not actively recruiting or selling. This addresses the problem of relying solely on active work for income.
  • High Cost of Telecom Services (Indirectly): While TGR doesn't directly lower the cost of airtime or data set by providers, the cashback and earnings from the platform effectively reduce the net cost of these services for active members.
  • Challenges of Traditional Network Marketing: By focusing on essential, constantly needed services rather than niche products, TGR potentially overcomes some of the difficulties associated with selling products and maintaining consistent demand.
  • Limited Wealth Distribution in the Telecom Sector: While companies like MTN generate huge profits and pay high salaries to a portion of their employees, TGR offers a way for everyday individuals to participate in and benefit financially from the vast wealth within the telecommunications industry.
  • Desire for Financial Empowerment and a Better Future: TGR offers hope and a tangible path for individuals to improve their financial situation, achieve financial goals, and potentially build a lasting legacy.
